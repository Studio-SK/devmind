# Design Process — Interview Phrases

## Opening Any Interview
"Before I start designing, let me clarify a few things to make 
sure I'm solving the right problem."

## After Clarifying
"Based on what you've told me, the core challenge here is 
[X] — let me do a quick capacity estimate to understand the scale 
before I draw anything."

## Transitioning To HLD
"Now that I have a sense of the scale, let me sketch out the 
high-level components first — I'll go deeper on the interesting 
parts once we agree on the overall shape."

## Proposing A Deep Dive
"I think the most interesting design challenge here is [X] — 
want me to go deeper on that?"

## Handling Unknowns
"I'm not deeply familiar with [X] — I know it's generally used 
for [purpose]. I'd approach it by [first principles]. 
Does that align with what you're looking for?"

## Wrapping Up
"To summarize — the main architectural decisions were [X, Y, Z], 
with the key tradeoffs being [A vs B]. Given more time I'd 
look at [next area to improve]."

## One-Liner
"I always clarify requirements and estimate scale before drawing 
anything — the numbers tell you what the architecture needs to solve."
